Chingri Malai Curry


Home

Description

Chingri Malai Curry is a rich and aromatic dish from the Bengal region, popular in both Bangladesh and West Bengal, India. This dish features succulent prawns (chingri) cooked in a velvety coconut milk gravy infused with fragrant spices. It is often served on special occasions such as weddings, family gatherings, and festivals like Eid and Pohela Boishakh.

The term "Malai" refers to cream, but in this dish, it signifies the luxurious texture of the coconut milk-based gravy rather than actual dairy cream. The dish has a delicate balance of sweetness from the coconut milk and a mild heat from green chilies, making it a perfect accompaniment to fragrant basmati rice or plain white rice.

Ingredients

  • 6 large prawns
  • 2 tbsp mustard oil
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 onion (finely chopped)

Steps

  1. Clean and devein prawns, then rub with turmeric and salt.
  2. Heat mustard oil in a pan and lightly fry the prawns. Set aside.
  3. In the same oil, fry onions until golden, then add ginger-garlic paste.
  4. Add prawns back into the pan and cook for 5 minutes.
  5. Sprinkle sugar and stir well. Serve hot with basmati rice.
