Description

Ilish Maach Bhaja (Hilsa Fish Fry) is a classic fish fry dish prepared in Bengali cuisine. It is prepared on all special occasion as fish is considered auspicious in Bengali culture. Hisla Fish Fry - Ilish Maach Bhaja doesn't really call for a recipe, but it definitely needs to be recorded and shared.

Ingredients

  • 8 steaks of hilsa fish
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric
  • ¼ cup mustard oil
  • ½ red onion
  • 2 fresh green chili

Steps

  1. Season the fish fillet with salt and turmeric and keep aside for 10 minutes.
  2. Pour the oil in a heavy bottom wok and let it heat until you see smoke. This is a very critical step. If the oil is not hot enough, and you drop the fish fillets, it will get stuck to the bottom of the pan.
  3. Slide the marinated fish very carefully in the hot oil.
  4. Let it fry for 2 minutes and the try to flip it very carefully. If it feels like it is stuck to the bottom of the pan, give it 1 to 2 more minutes for the fish to fry completely.
  5. Flip and fry for 2 more minutes.
  6. Take it off the pan once done and switch off the heat.
  7. The oil is still pretty hot, so drop the sliced red onion and slit green chili. Let it fry for 1 minute.
  8. Serve the pipping hot Ilish Maach Bhaja with steamed rice and top it with the cooked soft onion, green chili and some of that aromatic mustard oil.
  9. Enjoy while it's warm.
